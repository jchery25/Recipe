Pineapple Upside Down Pancakes

Serving ~ 5

Want to add a little tropical flavor and fun to your breakfasts? It’s easier than you might think, and it’s taking inspiration from an island-flavor dessert: pineapple upside-down cake! That’s right, we’re making Pineapple Upside Down Pancakes, and this easy, adaptable recipe is sure to delight anybody looking to start their day with something a little more special.

Ingredients

  • 2c Flour
  • 2tsp Baking Powder
  • 1½tsp Baking Soda
  • ½tsp Salt
  • ¼c Sugar
  • 1½c Milk
  • ½tsp Vanilla extract
  • ½c Butter, plus additional for frying
  • ¼c Brown sugar
  • 1½oz Spiced rum, optional
  • 20oz Pineapple rings with juice
  • 1cMaraschino cherries

Preparation

For Pinapple

①Melt butter with cinnamon stick in a large heavy skillet over medium heat

②Cook until butter begins to brown, about 2 minutes.

③Add pineapple slices; cook until light golden brown, 4-5 minutes per side.

④Remove skillet from heat; add rum and brown sugar.

⑤Cook over medium-high heat until juices are thick and syrupy.

⑥Transfer pineapple to a plate; let cool. Reserve syrup.

For Pancakes

①Preheat oven to 325°F. Set a wire rack on a rimmed baking sheet.

②Whisk first 4 ingredients in a large bowl. Add buttermilk and next 3 ingredients; whisk until smooth.

③Heat a griddle or large heavy skillet over medium heat. Lightly coat with nonstick spray.

④Working in batches, pour batter by 1/4-cupfuls onto griddle.

⑤Cook each pancake until golden brown and bubbles form on top, about 2 minutes.

⑥Top each pancake with a pineapple ring.

⑦Flip; cook until pancake is golden brown, about 2 minutes.

⑧Place pancakes on prepared rack and bake in oven until cooked through, about 5 minutes.

⑨Rewarm reserved pineapple syrup. Divide pancakes among plates; drizzle with pineapple syrup.
